Ophelia

A film by Lane Haven

The Film

Theatre moves.

"OPHELIA" follows River, a college student dealing with the loss of his sister Sarah, an upperclassman theatre major. He takes an acting class, not sure he wants to be there, but he stays.

As rehearsals go on, the grief he's been carrying starts to come out through the work. "Hamlet's" lines start to feel like his own. The stage becomes the one place he can't hide.

Set in 1998. Shot on a college campus. Character-driven, with flashes of surreal imagery where memory and performance blur into one another.

Vision

Hey y'all, my name is Lane Haven. I am an actor, director, and writer from Keller, Texas. I'm in my third year studying film and theatre at the University of Southern California. OPHELIA is my attempt to marry these two artistic mediums.

I have been doing theatre since I was in 6th grade. It was my first love and gave me a playground to express myself from a young age. I want to share this passion through storytelling itself, giving theatre a new look and hopefully sparking newfound interest to a larger audience through the medium of film.

OPHELIA is set in the 90's but is stylized through a fresh modern lens, inspired by the evocative power of live theatre. I want this film to move people in the way theatre breathes, lives, and flows. Theatre has always been a space for connection, creativity, and transformation, and I believe it can reach even more people when presented in a bold, contemporary way.
