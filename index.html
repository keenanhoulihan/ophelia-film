Ophelia

A short film by Lane Haven

The Film

Sometimes the truth hurts. Sometimes it heals.

Ophelia follows River, a withdrawn college student grappling with the recent death of his sister Sarah — a passionate actor who once anchored his world. When he reluctantly joins a theatre class she loved, River is paired with Jo: ambitious, sharp-tongued, and determined to land the lead.

As they rehearse scenes from Hamlet, River's buried grief begins to surface — blurring the line between performance and reality. What begins as obligation evolves into a deeply personal journey of healing, as River learns that true performance requires vulnerability. That letting go doesn't mean losing connection.

Set on a college campus in 1996. Grounded, character-driven — with moments of surreal imagery where the stage and the self become one.

Director's Vision

"Ophelia is a stage where loss performs itself night after night, even after the audience has gone home."

— Lane Haven, Writer & Director

I want to make theatre look cool — increasing accessibility to a larger audience. Reimagined through a modern lens, inspired by the evocative power of live performance. I want this film to move people in the way that breathes, lives, and flows like theatre.
